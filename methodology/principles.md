# Principles

These principles are non-negotiable for the MVP phase of AI-LAB.

1. Evidence before opinion.
   Important claims must be traceable to evidence.

2. Experiments before conclusions.
   When evidence is insufficient or contradictory, design an experiment instead of closing the conclusion.

3. Reasonable reproducibility before perfection.
   Experiments should be repeatable enough to be useful, without blocking work on excessive formalism.

4. Observed facts separated from recommendations.
   What happened and what we recommend doing are different kinds of knowledge.

5. Explicit uncertainty.
   "Unknown" is a valid state. Lack of knowledge must be recorded, not hidden.

6. OpenCode is the first case, not the center.
   The lab studies AI engineering architectures, not one tool only.

7. Keep methodology lightweight until practice demands more structure.
   Do not add layers just because they are conceptually attractive.

8. Methodology must fit the size of the question.
   Small questions need small evidence records. Large architectural claims need stronger validation.
