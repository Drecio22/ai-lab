# Glossary

## System

The main object under study, such as OpenCode, LiteLLM, Ollama, Claude Code, Cline, Cursor, or Windsurf.

## Tool

A capability used by an agent or model to interact with the environment, such as reading files, editing files, running commands, or calling APIs.

## Runtime

The environment that executes sessions, agents, tools, prompts, model calls, or workflows.

## Provider

A service or component that gives access to one or more models.

## Model

An AI model used to generate responses or perform reasoning.

## Agent

An entity configured with instructions, context, tools, permissions, and optionally a model.

## Subagent

An agent invoked by another agent, runtime, or delegation mechanism to perform a subtask.

## Router

A component or policy that decides which model, provider, agent, tool, or execution path to use.

## Gateway

An intermediate layer that normalizes, proxies, or controls access to providers or models.

## Experiment

A controlled procedure designed to test a hypothesis or clarify unknown behavior.

## Evidence

Information that supports, weakens, or refutes a claim. Evidence can come from source code, documentation, tests, releases, PRs, issues, discussions, community examples, or experiments.

## Claim

A verifiable technical statement with state, confidence, evidence, and limitations.

## Spec

A technical specification describing confirmed behavior, open questions, hypotheses, and limitations.

## ADR

An Architecture Decision Record. It records an important architectural decision, alternatives considered, and consequences.

## Pattern

A reusable architectural lesson that appears transferable beyond a single tool or study.

## Insight

A lightweight cross-cutting observation that may later become a pattern, claim, or recommendation.

## Observability

The ability to reconstruct what happened during a run: configuration, model, provider, tools, inputs, outputs, logs, timings, errors, and limitations.

## Observed Truth

Knowledge about what was observed, measured, or verified.

## Recommended Truth

Knowledge about what the lab recommends doing, based on observed truth, tradeoffs, and context.

## Unknown

An explicit state meaning the lab does not yet have enough evidence to support or reject a claim.
